Article Summary

Apple's newest iPhone has just gone on sale and you can now get your own new iPhone 11, iPhone 11 Pro or iPhone 11 Pro Max. Whether you use it as a personal device or professional hardware, the newest iPhones balance price and features to once again be the benchmark for all smartphones. Prices for the new iPhone 11 starts at $979.

Once again smartphone developers are playing catch-up to Apple's best iPhone just as they're ready to deliver a new model. This year they launched the iPhone 11 for consumers, and iPhone 11 Pro (and larger Pro Max) for people who want a device that blurs the lines between camera phone and professional camera.

It's not just a faster processor and more battery life (although those are indeed features of the iPhone 11 lineup). The multiple cameras zoom seamlessly between each other as any focus or colour balance you do on one lens is copied to the others, priming them for a sudden switch.

If cameras are awesome to you, but you'd rather save money then you'll be happy to know that the iPhone 8 and iPhone XR will be remaining on the market for another year. For the next few months you should also find the iPhone XS and iPhone XS Max on clearance prices.

The differences between the new iPhone 11 and last year's iPhone XR are minor, but we cover the changes and which iPhone is best for your needs in our iPhone 11 vs iPhone XR guide.

Apple iPhone 11 and iPhone 11 Pro Hardware Specs

In addition to supporting older devices on the market, Apple has introduced three new models. The iPhone 11 is an impressive replacement for the iPhone XR, and features premium features introduced with the iPhone XS and new hardware inside like the new A13 CPU. 

Even Better Performance: All three phones include the newest A13 Bionic CPU which improves both processing and graphics performance over last year's model, making it the fastest smartphone on the market including all those Android choices. The event also highlighted the new CPU's power-saving capabilities. When partnered with new battery tech, all three models feature up to 5 hours longer life than the 2018 iPhones.

Beautiful Look All Around: Prices range, but starting storage does not. Every base model includes 64GB of storage. Displays feature True Tone, Wide colour display and Haptic Touch for immediate feedback. The "notch" is still here too. While the iPhone 11 and iPhone 11 Pro models are wrapped in glass, the Pro models feature stainless steel design and matte glass finish while the iPhone 11 has a strong, aluminum frame. All new iPhone 11 devices are rated IP68 for dust and water resistance. 

Pro-Grade, Intelligent Cameras: The cameras on the iPhone 11 are an impressive 12MP wide angle paired with a 12MP ultra-wide angle lens. iPhone 11 Pro models add an extra 12MP telephoto lens. No matter how many you have, the camera software can seamlessly switch between them. Allowing smooth zoom transitions even further blurs the lines between a camera phone and a professional camera rig.

Connectivity: The newest Wi-Fi 6 tech is in here, as is Bluetooth 5.0. You'll have to wait another year before getting 5G on your iPhone. That's fine because 5G isn't coming to Canada until at least 2020.

Apple iPhone 11 Specs

While the iPhone 11 "only" features two rear-facing cameras, it comes in more colour variations than any model since the brilliant iPhone 5c. Unlike the iPhone 5c this is more than just last year's tech packaged in a pretty plastic case. The iPhone 11 partners some of the tech from last year's premium iPhone XR with the newest internals of Apple's 2019 flagship phone.

The Liquid Retina HD screen is a 6.1" IPS LCD screen and 1792x828 pixels (almost as good as a full HD screen). The aluminum frame surrounded by glass on both sides reveals solid blocks of white, black, (PRODUCT)RED, or a more pastel-like green, yellow or purple.

Apple iPhone 11 Pro and iPhone 11 Pro Max Specs

Many hardware features are shared between the iPhone 11 Pro and iPhone 11 Pro Max. The major difference being the screen (and case) size as well as battery life.

Industry-Leading Display: Both iPhone 11 Pro models feature a new OLED screen with 2,000,000:1 contrast ration over 450 pixels-per-inch (ppi) making it one of the sharpest iPhones ever. The old "Liquid Retina HD display" didn't sound impressive enough, so they call these "Super Retina XDR display". It's just a lot of words (and letters) to let you know they've improved this industry-leading display yet again. 

  • The iPhone 11 Pro has a 5.8" HDR screen with 2436x1125 resolution
  • The iPhone 11 Pro Max carries a 6.5" display and 2688x1242 resolution

Both are just under QuadHD resolution and produce 800 nits of brightness making this a brilliant screen for any situation indoors or out.

Even More Cameras: The iPhone 11 Pro and iPhone 11 Pro Max feature a third 12MP telephoto lens. The use of multiple lenses on the back is a popular trend (Samsung has a model with six total cameras!) but the seamless integration between the cameras is iPhone's best trick. You can carefully zoom between the three cameras just like a manual camera zoom, providing unique transitions that keep the viewer in the action rather than remind them you shot this on an iPhone. 

Sophisticated Case Design: The case design is much more sophisticated. Made from a solid piece of stainless steel then surrounded on both sides by glass, a matte finish gives the back a unique texture while letting through the silver, gold, space grey or midnight green body colour. If the three camera lenses aren't a dead giveaway that this is a premium device, the beautiful colour tones will.

Battery Life: Battery is improved over the iPhone XS, with up to 5 hours better performance than last year's flagship devices.

Release Date and Offers

The newest iPhone 11 smartphones hit the store shelves on September 20, 2019, so they're on sale now.

The iPhone XS, iPhone XS Max, and older iPhone 7 (and 7 Plus) have reached their end-of-life support. The iPhone 8 and last year's extremely popular iPhone XR will remain on the market for Apple-Faithful that want to save a few bucks. 

In the next few months we'll probably see some really great prices on the iPhone XS and iPhone 7 models as carriers liquidate their old stock. Visit and bookmark our Best iPhone Deals page regularly to see the best sales on every model of Apple iPhone in Canada.

Every carrier usually offers deals on the popular iPhone smartphones. WhistleOut helps you find the best.

Apple TV+ and Apple Arcade Subscriptions

Apple TV+ launches November 1 in over 100 countries, and is priced at a reasonable $5.99 CDN for your whole family. That's cheaper than Disney+ and way cheaper than Netflix. The first year is free when you buy a new Apple TV, Mac, iPad or iPhone. Jason Momoa stars in "See", one of the exclusive series launching on the platform and the trailer was launched at the Apple event. 

Also presented was Apple Arcade. With hundreds of games available within the first few weeks, Apple intends to add even more to the subscription every month. Also priced at a reasonable $5.99 CDN per month for everyone on your family account. 

Apple Arcade seems like a great way for casual gamers to keep fresh content on their device without being annoyed by ads, pay-to-play freemium games or paying for individual games. The service works with both the iPhone and iPad. Apple Arcade launched in 100+ countries on September 19th with a huge lineup. There are a few Apple Arcade games that were developed in Canada and worth a look.

Apple Watch Series 5

The Apple Watch segment began with heart-warming stories (pun intended) directly from people who were helped by the innovative ECG technology, fall detection, breathing reminders and more. Some had their lives potentially saved by the Apple Watch. This is one of the technologies that really impresses me as it makes tech that normally costs $1,000's more available for only a few hundred.

All day (18 hour) battery life has been the standard for Apple watches, but now the screen is always on. Previously you needed to raise your wrist to enable the display and see what's happening, but now it's on all the time and the display is visible from every direction. There's compass hardware built into the Apple Watch now, which could be invaluable when following directions.

Prices start at $529 CDN and are now available in Apple stores and select carriers alongside the new iPhone 11 models. At $659 CDN for the cellular model you can also access emergency services anywhere in the world there's a cell signal. The Series 3 will still be on the market and for sale at a discounted $259 CDN ($389 CDN with cellular capabilities).
